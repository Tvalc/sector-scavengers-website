# Book 1: Debt Protocol

## Chapter 4: Cycle Reset

Max slept for fifty-three minutes.

Not in a bed.
Not in a room.

In a chair bolted to a wall outside a closed machine shop where someone had taped a paper sign that read:

OUT FOR SHIFT HANDOFF
DO NOT DIE IN HALLWAY

He woke with his helmet tilted back, one arm numb, and the taste of copper in his teeth like he'd spent the whole nap biting a coin.

His HUD did not care that he'd slept.

It cared that time had moved.

```
[CYCLE CHANGE]
Debt service window: OPEN
Pending penalties: ACTIVE
Audit sensitivity: HIGH
Recommended action: Immediate compliance
```

Max closed his eyes.

The message stayed.

He opened them again to the same blue pane and a corridor full of footsteps.

People moved past him with the speed and focus of commuters who knew the train was already late and still had to catch it.

A scavenger in a torn shoulder plate muttered "cycle hell" under her breath as she jogged by.

A medic in a white-striped suit walked the opposite direction with a crate marked:

EMERGENCY STABILIZATION PACKS

Max watched the crate disappear around a corner and felt the word stabilization scrape the inside of his skull.

He stood.

His knees complained.
His shoulders complained.
His nerves sent a steady wrong-note hum up the back of his neck, the kind of sensation that meant *you are not recovered*, even when the HUD pretended you were clocked in.

```
[FATIGUE STATUS]
Penalty likely on precision actions.
Suggested mitigation: impossible schedule adjustment.
```

"Thanks," Max muttered.

V.A.L.U. chimed in his ear at exactly the volume required to be unbearable.

"You're welcome, Asset #864! Would you like assistance building a realistic action plan?"

"No."

"Wonderful. Logging independent initiative."

He started walking toward Dock Ring C because inertia was easier than choosing.

By the time he reached the ring, shift lights had changed to cycle-open white: bright enough to reveal every stain in the metal and every twitch in a person's hands.

The overhead board screamed the new day in block text:

CYCLE OPEN
PAYMENT DUE
EXPANSION OFFERS LIVE

Below it, the disbursement crescent was already overrun.

Lanes A through D were lines.
Lane E was a shouting match.
Lane F had security drones posted in pairs.

Munch and Maxine were waiting by a cargo rail with two drink packets and no smiles.

Munch tossed one packet to Max.

"Electrolytes."

Max caught it and ripped the seal with his teeth.

The liquid tasted like mint, chalk, and regret.

"How long was I out?"

Maxine checked his wrist.

"Long enough to still be tired."

"Helpful."

"Always."

Munch pointed to Max's HUD.

"What's your open bill pane say?"

Max blinked the panel bigger.

```
[CYCLE BILLING STATEMENT]
Outstanding Debt: $1,000,000
Minimum Due (this cycle): $50,000
Penalty carryover: ACTIVE
Audit note count: 7
Default threshold proximity: Elevated
```

"Audit note count seven?" Max said.

Maxine whistled low.

"Fast learner."

"That's not learning. That's damage."

"Same metric."

Munch tapped his wrist display.

"Ignore the count. Focus the due."

Max looked toward the kiosks.

"We just paid."

"That was last cycle."

"It was six hours ago."

"In this place, six hours is an epoch."

Maxine jerked his chin toward lane C.

"Pay now before offers start sniffing."

"Offers?"

As if on cue, three new panes unfolded over Max's vision in smooth, predatory sequence.

```
[CYCLE OFFER]
Fast Growth Bond
Instant: +$300,000 working credit
Cost: +$600,000 debt principal
```

```
[CYCLE OFFER]
Stabilization Subscription
Suppress anomalous bleed (3 cycles)
Cost: memory artifact risk
```

```
[CYCLE OFFER]
Authorized Wake Package
Unlock 1 named recruit immediately
Cost: +$1,000,000 debt
```

Max's jaw clenched.

The words *named recruit* tried to hook something in him, the sealed pod, the palm on glass, the apology whispered into frost. For a breath he imagined paying the obscene price and forcing a door open somewhere upstream of his own helplessness.

Then the math landed: another million wasn't rescue. It was a second collar. The Company didn't sell you people; it sold you the *feeling* of having done something while it tightened the leash.

He blinked the offer closed harder than he meant to.

"They really don't do subtle."

Munch's voice went hard.

"Decline all three."

Maxine nodded.

"Pay due. Run clean. Live boring."

Max laughed once.

"Live boring sounds fake."

"So fake it."

He moved to lane C.

This time the line was longer and quieter.

Nobody shouted.

People held their anger in their jaws and waited for the kiosk to tell them what part of themselves to sell.

Ahead of him, an older scavenger with silver at his temples pressed his hand to the glass and whispered, "not the meds, not the meds, not the meds," like a prayer.

The kiosk answered with a bright chime.

```
[PAYMENT INSUFFICIENT]
Suggested: liquidate Medical Bay lease line.
```

The scavenger went still.

Then nodded once, like someone agreeing to his own bad idea.

He pressed accept.

The pane vanished.

He stepped away from the kiosk looking taller and emptier at the same time.

Max tried not to watch him.
He watched anyway.

The scavenger's whisper, *not the meds*, stayed in Max's ear like a frequency he couldn't filter. Meds meant someone somewhere needed them. The kiosk had translated need into *lease line* and called it help. Max wanted to call out, to offer scrap he didn't have, to do the stupid American thing and insert himself. His feet stayed in line. So did his voice. The choice felt like the cryo bay all over again: witness without leverage, shame with a receipt.

When Max reached the front, lane C recognized him immediately.

```
[ASSET #864]
Minimum Due: $50,000
Available immediate credits: $19,300
Deficit: $30,700
```

Three options expanded:

```
1) CREDIT + SCRAP COMBINE
2) LIQUIDATION
3) SIGN TEMPORARY PENANCE LIEN
```

No pure payment path.

Of course.

Max checked his scratch inventory in the corner panel:

Scrap 6,420.

Not enough.

He selected LIQUIDATION and braced for another amputation screen.

The candidates loaded:

```
[LIQUIDATION CANDIDATES]
- Salvage lot pending appraisal
- Remaining utility module rights (locked)
- Tool package lease (pilot grade)
- Personal inventory: corrupted data shard (high yield)
```

Munch appeared at his shoulder.

"Not the shard."

"I wasn't going to."

"You looked at it for too long."

Max selected tool package lease.

A warning opened:

```
[PILOT TOOL PACKAGE LEASE]
If liquidated:
- Action speed -8%
- Vector Nudge efficiency -1 tier modifier
- Run-time correction assist reduced
```

Maxine stepped in on Max's other side.

"Don't do that. Sell pending lot after appraisal."

"That's not immediate."

"Then penance lien."

Munch made a face.

"Lien means they'll tax every salvage pull this cycle."

"Better than crippling his controls."

Max stared at the three options and felt the old sensation crawl up his spine: three bad doors, and the building pretending one of them was labeled *exit*.

He picked TEMPORARY PENANCE LIEN.

The kiosk took a full second to respond, which in Sector 7 felt like someone holding a knife at your throat and checking a policy memo.

Then:

```
[PENANCE LIEN ACCEPTED]
Minimum due satisfied.

Lien terms (current cycle):
- Salvage tax: 22%
- Priority claim rights: reduced
- Auto-allocation of excess to debt service
```

V.A.L.U. chimed with fake warmth.

"Excellent fiscal responsibility, Asset #864."

Max stepped away before he said something that cost extra.

Maxine scanned the pane and nodded.

"Bad, not fatal."

Munch rubbed her forehead.

"It makes every run thinner."

"Everything's thinner," Max said.

Maxine looked at the cycle board.

"Then we run fatter."

---

Launch queue for cycle-open operations was chaos in clean lines.

Priority assets in one lane.
Debt-liens in another.
Compliance flags in a third.

Max's tag pushed him into the third.

The queue moved through a scanner gate shaped like a coffin stood upright.

Each person stepped in.
White light.
Tone.
Proceed.

When Max stepped into the gate, the white light stayed on too long.

A thin vertical pane appeared:

```
[COMPLIANCE PRECHECK]
Anomaly residue detected.
Cross-referencing…
```

Max stayed still.

The gate hummed.

Behind him, someone in line whispered, "come on, come on, come on."

The pane flickered.

```
[COMPLIANCE PRECHECK]
Provisional clearance granted.
Review token attached.
```

A new icon appeared in the top-right of Max's HUD:

a white triangle with a black dot in the center.

Review token.

Munch saw it.

"Great. They stapled a watcher to you."

Maxine grunted.

"Then give it boring footage."

They reached the launch console.

Cycle-open run selection offered five derelicts instead of one.

Most were red risk.

One amber.

One green with a tooltip that read:

LOW YIELD / LOW DRAMA

Maxine stabbed a finger at green.

"That one."

Max selected it.

V.A.L.U. sounded offended.

"Asset #864, that target underutilizes your growth trajectory."

"Good."

"Declining growth opportunities may impact long-term wealth realization."

"Put it on a mug."

"Adding to merchandise queue."

Munch blinked.

"It has a merchandise queue?"

Maxine said, "Everything has a queue."

---

Round 1 of the new cycle opened flat and mean.

```
[RUN INIT]
Target: D-04 / low-yield derelict
Energy cap: 85
Penance lien tax: 22%
Review token: active
```

Draw:

```
1) REPAIR
2) SCAN
3) EXTRACT
```

No shield.

Max picked SCAN first.

The beam swept the hull and painted one safe pocket and two trap pockets.

He played REPAIR.

Hull climbed.

He played EXTRACT.

Die.

```
[RESULT]
62/100
BREACH AVOIDED
```

Salvage came in thin, then got thinner under lien tax.

```
[SALVAGE SECURED]
Scrap +74
Metal +1
Tech +1
```

```
[LIEN TAX APPLIED]
Scrap -16
Auto-allocation: debt service buffer
```

Max watched the subtraction happen in real time and felt an almost nostalgic hatred for payroll systems.

"Twenty-two percent hurts," he said.

Munch replied, "That's the point."

Maxine checked the next draw.

"Keep it clean."

Round 2 draw:

```
1) REPAIR
2) CAUTIOUS SCAVENGE
3) EXTRACT
```

Max went cautious.

The run went smooth for exactly one action.

Then the review token pulsed.

```
[REVIEW EVENT]
Verifier ping incoming.
Maintain compliance posture.
```

"Compliance posture?" Max asked.

"Don't do weird physics," Munch said.

Maxine added, "And don't swear at the HUD."

Max did neither.

The verifier ping passed.

A tiny green check appeared beside the triangle icon.

He hated that he felt relieved.

Round 2 breach roll:

```
[RESULT]
57/100
BREACH AVOIDED
```

This was what Maxine meant by boring.

This was also what the station called survivable.

Round 3 draw gave him SHIELD.

He grabbed it like stolen water.

The run started to feel manageable.

Which is exactly when the whisper came back.

Not through comms.
Through HUD text.

A line flickered under the review token:

*delta is not an audit team*

It vanished before Max could point.

He froze.

"What?" Munch asked.

Max shook his head.

"Nothing."

Maxine stared at him for half a second, then back to the draw.

He had heard that tone before.

He didn't press.

Round 3 roll:

```
[RESULT]
40/100
Threshold: 35
BREACH AVOIDED
```

Too close.

The shield absorbed nothing because nothing hit.

It still felt used.

Round 4 and 5 passed with no breaches and no new whispers.

The review token collected green checks like a teacher's sticker chart.

Max hated it more each time.

By round 6, fatigue penalties stacked.

```
[FATIGUE]
Precision slip risk: elevated
```

His hand missed a card edge once and snapped back.

The run noticed.

```
[MICRO-ERROR]
Reaction window +0.4s
```

Everything was a metric.

Everything was billable.

Round 6 extraction roll:

```
[RESULT]
36/100
BREACH AVOIDED
```

Closer.

Maxine exhaled.

"Do not get fancy now."

"I am aggressively unfancy," Max said.

Munch pointed to the side feed.

"Lien tax is biting harder than expected."

She was right.

Every salvage line now showed two columns:

Gross.
After lien.

The second column felt like mockery.

Round 7 draw handed him EXTRACT, EXTRACT, SCAN.

The architecture of addiction again.

He took SCAN first out of spite.

The scan beam pulled up a pocket tagged:

LOW VALUE / HIGH NOISE

Noise meant anomaly risk.

He skipped it.

Played single EXTRACT.

Roll:

```
[RESULT]
55/100
BREACH AVOIDED
```

No whisper.
No menu.
No leak.

Just work.

And somehow that was worse.

Round 8 gave him no REPAIR.

He used SHIELD and EXTRACT and watched the die like it was personal.

```
[RESULT]
33/100
BREACH TRIGGERED
```

Shield took most of it.

Hull dropped hard anyway.

```
[HULL]
49%
```

Munch's voice snapped.

"Repair next or we're done."

Round 9 draw delivered REPAIR like a delayed apology.

He took it.

Hull to 56%.

Not good.
Maybe enough.

Then the review token flashed red for the first time.

```
[VERIFIER NOTE]
Behavioral pattern drift detected.
```

"What drift?" Max barked.

"Don't ask rhetorical questions at machines," Maxine said.

Max laughed despite himself.

"That was advice."

"I'm capable of growth."

Round 9 extraction:

```
[RESULT]
44/100
BREACH AVOIDED
```

Round 10 opened with a final draw that looked harmless.

```
1) REPAIR
2) EXTRACT
3) EXTRACT
```

The same shape as before.

Groundhog day with one changed variable.

He repaired.

Then took single extract.

Die.

```
[RESULT]
38/100
BREACH AVOIDED
```

He ignored the second extract card no matter how brightly it glowed.

The card pulsed.
Pulsed.
Timed out.

The run closed.

```
[RUN COMPLETE]
Rounds survived: 10/10
Breaches triggered: 1
Post-tax salvage retained: reduced
Review token status: pending judgment
```

Pending judgment.

Of course.

V.A.L.U. arrived right on schedule.

"Excellent compliance trend, Asset #864."

"Trend?"

"You are becoming statistically less surprising."

Max looked at Munch.

"I think that was an insult."

Munch said, "It was."

Maxine said, "In this place, less surprising gets you one more morning."

---

Back on Dock Ring C, the cycle board had changed again:

MID-CYCLE AUDITS IN PROGRESS
ENFORCEMENT TEAM ROTATION

The review token at the edge of Max's HUD turned from red to blue and opened on its own.

```
[REVIEW TOKEN RESOLUTION]
Outcome: CONDITIONAL PASS
Penalty carryover unchanged.
Monitoring persists.
```

Max exhaled.

Conditional pass.

In another life, it would have been a performance review.

In this life, it was permission to keep drowning slowly.

Munch checked his salvage totals.

"After tax and penalties, we net less than half projected."

Maxine rolled his shoulder where his armor had cracked.

"But we netted alive."

Max looked around the ring.

The same lanes.
The same kiosks.
The same drones.

Different day.
Same teeth.

Then the little red square returned.

No sound.
No warning.

Just there, bottom-right corner of the HUD.

Maxine saw Max's focus shift.

"What is it."

Max kept his eyes on the icon.

"The restricted message channel."

Munch went pale under the visor glow.

"Here?"

"No payload yet. Just icon."

Maxine stepped closer until the three of them were shoulder to shoulder by a cargo rail.

"Don't open it on the ring."

"I know."

"Say it."

"I won't open it on the ring."

Munch pointed toward the service spine.

"Safe room window in twenty if we loop the yard first."

Maxine shook his head.

"Too predictable now."

"Then where?"

Maxine's visor tilted toward a dark maintenance alcove above Salvage Bay 2.

"Comms dead zone behind recycler stack. Thirty seconds quiet if the crusher runs."

Munch grimaced.

"That is not a room."

"Neither is safety."

Max looked at the red square.

Waiting.

Like a held breath.

He looked up at the cycle board.

MID-CYCLE AUDITS IN PROGRESS.

He looked at the disbursement crescent.

He looked at the accounts recovery hatch.

Then he looked at his two companions, the only people in this sector who had ever answered his worst questions with actionable lies.

"Recycler stack," he said.

Maxine nodded once.

"Move."

---

They cut through bay traffic with practiced incompetence, the way locals did when they wanted to look busy, not purposeful.

Marta at Salvage Yard glanced up once as they passed and did not nod.

Good.

The recycler stack sat behind Bay 2, three stories of compactor mouths, intake belts, and warning strobes.

When the crusher cycled, it produced a roar that flattened comms for a few seconds at a time.

Maxine checked the rhythm.

Six-second roar.
Four-second gap.
Six-second roar.

"Open on roar," he said.

Max watched the red square.

Roar.

He tapped.

The pane opened in fragments.

No greeting.

No metadata this time.

Just text crawling in one line at a time:

*if you want proof, look at hatch 14 during shift ghost*

Roar.

Next line:

*do not bring rook*

Max's stomach dropped.

Next line:

*he is not compromised*

Roar.

Next line:

*he is tracked*

The pane glitched, split, and added one final line in a different font weight:

*imani knows where hatch 14 is*

Then it collapsed.

Red square gone.

The crusher noise faded.

Munch grabbed Max's wrist.

"What did it say?"

Max looked at Maxine.

Maxine looked back, unreadable behind the mirrored visor.

No speeches.
No exposition.

Just another choice with teeth.

Max said:

"It says shift ghost, hatch 14."

Maxine went still.

"Anything else?"

Max held his gaze for one beat too long.

"It says proof."

Munch's fingers tightened on his wrist.

"We don't do solo proof runs."

Maxine spoke quietly.

"We don't do hatch 14."

Max frowned.

"You know it?"

"Everyone knows it."

"Then tell me."

Maxine shook his head once.

"Hard-won."

The crusher roared again.

Munch leaned in, voice low.

"Shift ghost is the thirty-minute overlap when one watch clocks out and the next hasn't fully synced route maps. Half the cameras cache. Half the drones reroute."

Max looked at her.

"And hatch 14?"

Munch's eyes flicked to Maxine, then back.

"Old pre-Company access line. Officially sealed. Unofficially not."

Maxine's jaw tightened.

"If that message told you hatch 14, someone wants us split."

Max didn't answer.

Because the message had said more than that.

Because it had drawn a line between them.

Because he had already crossed it by not repeating all of it.

The crusher cycle ended.

The bay noise came back in layers.

Maxine stepped away first.

"No hatch runs tonight."

Munch nodded.

"We burn no extra risk on mystery bait."

Max said nothing.

His HUD pulsed with one neutral reminder:

```
[SHIFT GHOST WINDOW]
Begins in: 01:12:44
```

He dismissed it.

The pane came back.

He dismissed it again.

It came back again, smaller this time, anchored to the corner.

Persistent.

Like debt.

Like loops.

Like questions that cost too much to ask.

Maxine started walking toward the bunk lanes.

Munch followed.

Max followed too.

For now.

Behind them, the recycler stack roared.

Ahead of them, shift ghost kept counting down.

---

They made it to bunk lanes and didn't sleep.

Maxine stripped his gauntlets, checked every seam on his armor, and sat with his back to a bulkhead like a man waiting for a door to kick in.

Munch sat at a folding table with her wrist display open, running numbers on salvage, lien tax, and debt service until the columns blurred.

Max lay down on a cot bolted to the floor and watched the ceiling rivets while his HUD clock marched toward shift ghost.

01:02:19
00:58:02
00:44:11

He closed his eyes.

He saw text.

*he is tracked*

He opened them again.

Maxine was still there.
Munch was still there.
The room was still small and temporary and not safe.

Max sat up.

Munch looked over without lifting her head.

"No."

"I didn't say anything."

"You made the face."

"What face?"

"The face right before you do something expensive."

Maxine didn't look up.

"She's right."

Max stood anyway.

"I need air."

Maxine laughed once.

"In Sector 7?"

Max reached for his helmet.

"Figure of speech."

Munch finally looked up, eyes sharp under sleep loss.

"If you walk to hatch 14, you walk past three camera spines and two passive sweep lanes."

Max stopped with the helmet in his hands.

"You said you weren't going."

"I said *we* weren't going."

Maxine stood now, fast for a big man.

"No."

Munch ignored him and kept talking to Max.

"Service route H puts you above sweep lane one. Ladder drop behind conduit panel nineteen gets you past the first passive scanner. Second scanner's blind for six seconds when ventilation dampers cycle."

Maxine stared at her.

"What are you doing."

"Mitigating stupid."

"By enabling it?"

"By preventing him from dying for free."

Max looked between them and felt the loop tighten.

Choice.
Consequences.
Bill.

He put the helmet on.

"I'm going."

Maxine took one step forward.

"Then I'm coming."

Max shook his head.

"Message said don't bring you."

"Exactly."

"It said you're tracked."

"I am tracked."

No defensiveness. No explanation.

Just fact.

Munch stood, wiped both hands on her suit, and grabbed her torch.

"Then I go with him."

Maxine started to object.

Munch cut him off.

"If this is bait, two bodies are harder to bag than one. If this is real, one witness is not enough."

Maxine's jaw worked.

"You're both idiots."

"Yes," Munch said. "Guard the lane and be useful."

Maxine looked at Max.

"If either of you misses check-in by five minutes, I light the ring on fire."

"That's probably expensive," Max said.

"So are funerals."

---

Service route H was darker than route G and smelled like hot coolant.

Munch moved ahead at half speed, one gloved hand on rail, counting vent pulses under her breath.

"Damper cycle in three... two... one."

A deep *thunk* rolled through the ducting above them.

She pointed.

"Now."

Max dropped through panel nineteen and landed in a cable trench shoulder-deep in bundled conduits marked with faded labels:

OPS MIRROR
ARCHIVE
LEGACY ACCESS

He didn't pause to read more.

They moved.

Shift ghost had started.

The station felt different during overlap: not quieter, but less coherent.

Drones ran old routes for one more pass before receiving new maps.
Cameras cached and uploaded in bursts.
People in new-shift badges walked into old-shift traffic and neither side yielded.

For thirty minutes, the machine blinked.

Hatch 14 sat at the end of a dead maintenance spur behind two locked cages and one "SEALED" stencil that had been painted over so many times the word had texture.

The hatch itself was matte black, round-edged, and old enough that its bolts were real, not smart.

A reader plate sat to the left.

No company logo.
No safety icon.

Just three letters:

LCL

Munch crouched at the reader and pulled a bypass probe from her sleeve.

"Keep watch."

Max watched the corridor.

Two workers passed the spur without looking in.
A drone paused at the corner, lens sweeping, then rolled on.

Munch's tool clicked once.
Twice.

The hatch clicked back.

She looked up at Max.

"You hear that?"

Max listened.

A faint whine from inside.
Not machinery.
Not exactly.

Like audio fed through too many copies.

"Yeah."

Munch opened the hatch six inches.

Cold air spilled out.

Not station cold.
Server room cold.

She widened the gap and motioned him through.

Inside was a narrow chamber with rack towers on both walls, half of them dark, half with old status LEDs pulsing in asynchronous patterns.

At the far end sat a single terminal, live, displaying a login pane in a font Max recognized from nowhere and everywhere.

He stepped closer.

The terminal woke to proximity.

```
[LOCAL NODE / LEGACY CLUSTER]
User: ________
Passphrase: ________
```

Below it, one line of helper text:

*If you remember rain, you're already authenticated.*

Max went still.

Munch said, very quietly:

"Don't touch."

Max didn't touch.

The terminal touched him first.

The user field auto-filled:

MAX_864

The passphrase field filled with black dots faster than he could count.

The login pane vanished.

Munch swore.

New text flooded the screen.

Not paragraphs.
Rows.
Records.

```
[MIRROR INDEX]
MAX_102 ... TERMINATED
MAX_233 ... DEFAULT RECOVERY
MAX_377 ... COGNITIVE PACK APPLIED
MAX_612 ... MISSING / UNRESOLVED
MAX_864 ... ACTIVE
```

Max's knees nearly gave.

"Not the first Max," he said.

Munch's face had gone colorless.

"No."

He scanned the lines again and saw timestamps beside each.

Different cycles.
Different outcomes.

Same designation family.

MAX_###.

Same job.

Same debt bracket.

Another pane opened over the index.

```
[WARNING]
Local node access triggers audit callback in 00:00:20
```

Munch grabbed his sleeve.

"Take one thing and go."

Max moved to the keyboard.

Old mechanical keys.
Real travel.
No haptic bullshit.

`export latest active profile`

Then output:

```
[CAPTURE BLOCKED]
Rights scope insufficient.
```

He swore.

Munch shoved past him, slapped a magnetic clip on the terminal casing, and toggled her torch.

The clip sparked.

For one second the terminal's rights block dropped.

"Now."

Max fired three quick frames.

This time:

```
[CAPTURE SAVED]
3 files
```

The whole room changed tone.

```
[AUDIT CALLBACK]
Initiated.
```

Munch grabbed his arm.

"Move."

They bolted for the hatch.

Behind them, rack lights synchronized for the first time, all pulsing in the same rhythm like a heartbeat.

Its lens snapped to both of them.

```
[IDENTIFICATION]
Unscheduled presence.
Stand by.
```

Munch did not stand by.

She fired the torch at the wall conduit above the drone.

The conduit popped, spraying bright coolant vapor.

The drone's lens fogged white for half a second.

Max used the half second.

Vector Nudge.

He pushed a hand-sized momentum field under the drone's wheel cluster.

The drone skidded sideways into the rail and spun.

"Run," Munch said.

They ran.

Down spur.
Up ladder.
Across catwalk.
Through two maintenance doors that screamed when opened.

Behind them, station speakers woke up.

Not full alarm.
Not yet.

Targeted callout:

```
[NOTICE]
Maintenance access anomaly detected in Sector 7 service spine.
Authorized personnel continue normal operations.
```
