# Book 1: Debt Protocol

## Chapter 14: Side Effect Sunrise

Morning in Sector 7 didn’t arrive with sun.

It arrived with receipts.

Some receipts were paper.

Some were hashes.

Some were blood pressure graphs pretending to be morale.

Max preferred the hashes.

Hashes didn’t flatter you.

Not yet.

The service spine didn’t brighten.

It *relented.*

Shift ghost was never kindness, just a dimmer switch the station used when it wanted the workforce to feel like night belonged to them, then quietly steal it back at handoff.

Max’s boots found rhythm on grated decking: not fast, not slow.

The rhythm of a man who knew cameras loved panic and hated patience.

Maxine walked like a door.

Munch walked like a lawyer who’d learned litigation in hallways because courtrooms were for people who could afford air.

Behind them, the trunk’s hatch sealed with a chirp that sounded too cute for what it guarded.

Relay nine stayed in the dark.

DEFERRED, the state had said.

Deferred was a word that pretended to be mercy.

Max knew better.

Deferred was a loan with teeth.

Max had learned the taste of that word in smaller bites: the cryo glass he couldn't open, the strut he didn't report, the tether he didn't cut. *Deferred* always meant someone else paid interest first, until the account rolled over and your name was next.

His HUD woke the way debt always woke, before his brain finished arguing with his body.

```
[PUBLICATION EVENT]
artifact: SIDE_EFFECT_MATRIX (public caches)
status: ACKNOWLEDGED
countermeasures: PENDING
public verification: RETAINED
note: interest accrual unaffected
```

Acknowledged.

Pending.

Retained.

Three words that didn’t agree with each other.

Munch’s tablet vibrated once, a harsh buzz, not a polite ping.

She didn’t look down immediately.

Looking down was a tell.

She waited until a junction blind spot, then angled the screen so only their crew triangle could read:

```
[LEGAL ROUTER]
rider publication clause: SATISFIED (matrix)
executive response packet: FILED
classification: REMEDIATION / NARRATIVE HYGIENE
```

Narrative hygiene.

Max tasted the phrase like copper.

"They’re going to wash the crowd," he muttered.

"They’re going to wash *us*," Maxine said.

Munch’s mouth tightened.

"They’re going to try," she said.

```

```

Ongoing costs were the station’s favorite subscription.

Max thought of the fatigue strut he hadn’t reported, another ongoing cost, paid in someone else’s future. SHIELD couldn’t patch metal. It could only patch *him*, which was the whole joke.

Maxine muttered:

"They’re mixing propaganda into the HVAC."

"Everything is a broadcast lane," Max said.

"Welcome to Sector 7," Maxine replied.

A maintenance bot rolled past with a happy face painted on its hazard shell.

The face was peeling.

Peeling was honest.

Max almost saluted it.

Saluting was a profile.

The corridor widened into a worker chokepoint, same architecture as always, different weather.

Not temperature.

Attention.

Tablets glowed in hands like campfires.

`SIDE EFFECTS ARE FEATURES`

Someone else had crossed out *features* and written *receipts* underneath.

A third person, kid, maybe sixteen if the station counted years honestly, held a phone aimed at nothing, recording everything.

Recording was prayer.

Max didn’t stop for interviews.

Interviews were traps with microphones.

He nodded once at the kid anyway.

Small.

Human.

Not performative enough to become a clip title.

The kid’s eyes went shiny.

Hope was a liability.

Max hated that he’d contributed.

He kept walking.

A guard drone drifted overhead, lens sweeping in slow arcs.

It paused on Max’s audit tab, heartbeat mirrored, destination unknown, *known enough to be a leash.*

Max used Vector Nudge Tier 1 on the drone’s stabilization gyro, micro tick, two points, enough to make it overshoot by six inches and re-target the wrong cluster of workers like they were the interesting threat.

The drone corrected.

By then, Max was past.

Not invisible.

Just inconvenient.

Munch whispered:

"That’s going to show up as 'anomalous interference.'"

"Anomaly is accurate," Max said.

Checksums.

Honest.

Max’s heart did something stupid and warm.

He didn’t intervene.

Intervention turned education into spectacle.

He walked through the argument like it was weather.

Weather was democracy at scale.

`Checksum: 0xR910-01A0`

Unsure was also the only option that didn’t hand the station a clean moral graph.

```

```

"Suppress secondary prompts pending review," she said.

`SECONDARY PROMPTS: DEFERRED`

Deferred again.

The station’s favorite fake mercy.

Behind them, a compliance attendant smiled with eyes that didn’t.

"Thank you for participating in trust-building!"

Trust-building.

Max nodded once, polite enough to deny intent.

Polite was armor.

Armor scratched.

`ATTESTATION: WITHDRAWAL PENDING`

It was still better than sealed.

Munch looked back like he was a client.

Clients kept you honest.

Max stepped close enough to murmur:

"Next time, choose UNSURE until you see a checksum."

"Unsure feels like cowardice," he whispered.

"Unsure is accuracy," Max said.

Accuracy was the only courage that scaled.
