# Book 1: Debt Protocol

## Chapter 3: Audit Teeth

The gap didn't blink.

It waited.

Max's finger hovered over the edge of his HUD where the choice layer had peeled open, just enough to reach.

Not a menu.
Not a promise.

A door.

Munch's hand closed over his wrist.

"Don't," she said. "You already fed it a breach."

He jerked back.

The phantom shimmer thinned, not gone, but less hungry.

The skiff still hummed under him. Dock Ring C slid past the canopy in slow strips of light and shadow. The autopilot had the stick now: not a request, a route.

```
[ROUND 4/10  /  LOGGED]
Incident: breach (contained)
Crew loss: none
Hull integrity: 66%
Audit signature: unstable
```

**Audit signature unstable.** The phrase sat in his skull like grit.

Munch was still leaning close, one hand braced on the console, torch holstered but not relaxed.

Maxine stood behind the pilot seat, one gauntlet on the overhead frame, helmet reflecting blue panes that kept trying to become instructions.

"That was round four," Maxine said.

"I noticed," Max replied.

"No second draw on this leg," Munch said. "The System wants us on the ground before it rolls another check."

Max didn't trust that mercy. The station didn't cancel rounds out of kindness, it canceled them because it had something worse waiting in the queue.

A new pane replaced the gap. Not with cards. With a door.

```
[ENFORCEMENT WINDOW OPEN]
Minimum due required now.
Proceed to DISBURSEMENT / PENANCE.
Delay will trigger active compliance measures.
```

Munch's jaw tightened.

Maxine muttered, "Here we go."

Max stared at the words until they blurred and then sharpened again.

"How active are active compliance measures?"

Munch answered without looking at him.

"Depends how expensive your lesson needs to be."

The skiff detached and auto-routed toward Dock Ring C. Not requested. Not negotiated. Routed.

V.A.L.U. slipped into the silence, unusually soft.

"Asset #864, congratulations on round four completion. Please report for financial wellness processing."

"Financial wellness," Max said.

"We care about your future."

"No, you care about your ledger."

"Our goals are aligned."

"They are not."

V.A.L.U. paused for half a beat.

"Feedback logged as aspirational."

---

The dock was louder during enforcement windows.

Not because alarms were louder.

Because people were.

Shouting at kiosks.
Shouting at drones.
Shouting at themselves for not having more scrap, more cards, more time.

The DISBURSEMENT / PENANCE banks were arranged in a crescent, each kiosk fronted by a line that moved too quickly for comfort and too slowly for mercy.

Above them, an overhead board scrolled status in white-on-blue:

PAYMENT DUE
DEFAULT PENDING
COMPLIANCE ACTIVE

Max stepped off the skiff and felt the station gravity settle wrong in his knees. The neural feedback hum behind his eyes had become a constant now. Not pain. Occupancy.

Maxine pointed toward the third kiosk in the crescent.

"Use lane C. Fewer auditors."

"Why fewer?"

"Because lane C likes clean defaults. Less paperwork."

Munch shot him a look. "Not helping."

Max moved anyway.

At lane C, an asset in front of him slammed both palms against the glass.

"I paid last cycle. I paid!"

The kiosk replied in neutral tone.

```
[ACCOUNT REVIEW]
Previous payment acknowledged.
Current insufficiency remains actionable.
```

The asset laughed once, ragged.

"Actionable? What does that even mean?"

A compliance drone glided in from the side, lens already focused.

"It means step back," Munch whispered.

The asset didn't.

The drone extended a thin armature with a contact pad at the end. It pressed the pad to the asset's wrist display. There was a bright white flash and a smell like burnt plastic.

The asset dropped to one knee, gasping, clutching his arm.

The kiosk chimed pleasantly.

```
[ENCOURAGEMENT DELIVERED]
Please continue.
```

Max didn't realize he had taken a step backward until his calves hit the queue rail.

Then forward again, stupid, honest muscle memory.

Maxine's gauntlet caught him at the sternum, gentle only in the way a doorstop was gentle.

"Don't," Maxine murmured. "You kneel beside him, you become a second exhibit. They love exhibits in pairs."

Max's voice came out rough. "He's hurt."

"He's reminded," Maxine said. "Different department. Same fist."

The man on one knee tried to stand and failed once. The kiosk chirped again, patient as a predator fed on schedule.

Max met his eyes for the half second the man could stand to hold them. There was no thank-you in it. There was *survive*, and *don't make this worse*, and something that looked like shame for needing the warning.

Max swallowed. "What did it do to him?"

Maxine didn't move his hand until Max stopped pushing.

"Reminded him he owes."

The line moved.

Max moved with it.

He carried the man's gasp in his ears the way he still carried the thumping glass: evidence he wasn't allowed to file.

By the time he reached the kiosk, his pulse had found a hard steady rhythm: the kind that meant panic had been promoted to operations.

The glass lit.

```
[ASSET #864]
OUTSTANDING DEBT: $1,000,000
MINIMUM DUE: $50,000
AVAILABLE SCRAP CONVERSION: $23,400 equivalent
DEFICIT: $26,600
```

Below it, three options unfolded.

```
1) PAY PARTIAL + ACCEPT PENALTY
2) SIGN PENANCE CONTRACT
3) EMERGENCY LIQUIDATION (ASSET LIST)
```

Max stared at option three.

Emergency liquidation.

The words sounded like surgery with no anesthesia.

He tapped it.

A sub-menu opened.

```
[LIQUIDATION CANDIDATES]
- Stored salvage package (low)
- Utility module lease rights (medium)
- Personal inventory item: Unlabeled data shard (corrupted) (high)
```

Munch made a noise in her throat.

"No."

Max didn't look away from the pane. "You said don't open the shard."

"I said don't open it. Not sell it."

"Why?"

Munch pressed her lips together.

For a second Max thought she would explain.

She didn't.

"Because some things cost more after you let go."

Maxine tapped the rail with one gauntlet finger.

"She's right. Sell the module rights first if you must. You can rebuild those slower than you can rebuild whatever that shard is."

Max scanned the numbers.

Selling module lease rights would cover the deficit and then some.

It would also tag his account with infrastructure loss for upcoming rounds.

The system helpfully simulated outcomes:

```
[PROJECTED EFFECTS]
If liquidate module rights:
- Next two rounds: Energy cap -15%
- Upgrade access: restricted
- Audit sensitivity: +1 tier
```

Max laughed under his breath.

"It's always plus one tier."

V.A.L.U. answered instantly.

"Tier progression is a growth opportunity."

"You're a parasite with branding."

"I can hear stress in your voice. Would you like an inspirational quote?"

"No."

"Delivering quote anyway for retention metrics: 'Discomfort is data.'"

Max's jaw locked. *Data* was what you called a person when you wanted permission to stop treating them like one.

He almost tapped the shard anyway, high yield, clean math, a single gesture that would make the deficit vanish. Munch's *no* hung in his ear. So did the sealed pod's thumping, useless as that was: some doors you didn't open even when the menu offered you a price.

Max selected utility module lease rights.

The kiosk paused.

For the first time since he woke, a loading bar appeared with no percentages, just a white line that grew and then stalled and then grew again.

The line finished.

```
[LIQUIDATION COMPLETE]
Credit applied: $28,000
Minimum due satisfied.
Excess applied to penalty buffer.

ACCOUNT SIDE EFFECTS:
- Energy cap reduction active (2 rounds)
- Upgrade restrictions active
- Audit sensitivity increased
```

The glass displayed a smiling icon that looked nothing like a face.

```
[THANK YOU FOR CHOOSING RESPONSIBILITY]
```

Max stepped away from the kiosk before he put his fist through it.

Munch matched his pace.

Maxine fell in on the other side.

No one spoke until they reached a dead comms alcove between two storage bays where the station noise dipped from roar to growl.

Munch turned on him.

"Don't do that again."

"Do what?"

"Liquidate infrastructure for a short-term patch."

Max stared at her.

"I paid."

"You amputated."

The word landed where *traitor* would have landed back home, not to other people, to himself. He had traded future capacity for present breathing room. That was rational. That was also how institutions preferred you: always one emergency away from the next bad menu.

"I didn't touch the shard," Max said, quieter.

Munch's shoulders eased a fraction. "That matters."

"It doesn't feel like enough."

"It never does," Maxine said. "Enough is a story they sell you after they take the first bite."

Maxine lifted a hand.

"Both true. Save the yelling for when yelling changes outcomes."

Munch blew out a breath and scrubbed a gloved hand over her faceplate.

"Fine. Round five, we play conservative and rebuild cap."

Max blinked. "Round five?"

Maxine snorted.

"You thought payment ended the day?"

Max looked back toward the kiosk crescent. Another asset was on his knees now. Another drone was calm about it.

"No," Max said. "I just forgot to hope less."

---

The next launch came faster than felt legal.

Round five opened with a restricted draw.

```
[ROUND 5/10 READY]
Energy cap adjusted: 85
Audit sensitivity: HIGH
Choose action.
```

Card spread:

```
1) SCAN
2) REPAIR
3) CAUTIOUS SCAVENGE
```

Munch pointed.

"REPAIR first. Keep the hull above sixty and don't trigger anything weird."

Maxine added, "No deep scans. No menu touching. No heroics."

Max picked REPAIR and felt weirdly offended by how much relief that gave him.

Microdrones patched.

Hull climbed.

Everything looked almost manageable for eleven full seconds.

Then the comms whispered.

Not V.A.L.U.

Not static.

A voice shredding at the edges, whole syllables missing.

"...right lane... don't..."

Max froze.

Maxine saw it instantly.

"Don't chase it."

Munch toggled local comm filters and cursed.

"It's piggybacking on tactical channel."

The whisper came again, closer.

"...he sold... the wrong..."

Max's skin crawled.

"Did you hear that?"

Munch didn't answer.

Maxine answered too quickly.

"No."

Which meant yes.

A panel snapped open in Max's HUD.

```
[AUDIO ANOMALY]
Restricted stream attached.
Action: BLOCK / TRACE
```

Max knew the right answer.

He selected BLOCK.

The panel vanished.

The whisper did not.

It got quieter.
Not gone.
Just farther back, like someone speaking from behind a locked door.

"Good choice," Munch said.

"Didn't work."

"Different things."

The derelict rotated through the canopy and presented a fresh seam: narrow, ragged, almost invitation-shaped.

Max dragged CAUTIOUS SCAVENGE.

The extractor extended.

Clamps bit.

Risk bars climbed.

Then another hazard draw hit.

```
[DANGER EVENT]
COMPLIANCE MIRROR
Effect: Duplicate last successful tactic with reduced efficiency.
Penalty: Additional audit logging.
```

"What does that do?" Max asked.

"Whatever hurts and files paperwork," Maxine said.

The system duplicated his last REPAIR effect at forty percent output.

Tiny gain.
Big log.

```
[AUDIT NOTE ADDED]
Pattern irregularity retained for review.
```

Max swore.

The hull held, barely.

He triggered EXTRACT before the mirror could stack another note.

```
[BREACH CHECK]
Rolling...
```

Die.

Pause.

```
[RESULT]
47/100
Threshold: 35
BREACH AVOIDED
```

No celebration.

Just motion.

```
[SALVAGE SECURED]
Scrap +98
Metal +2
Tech +2
XP +16
```

Then:

```
[ACCOUNT ALERT]
Audit sensitivity remains HIGH.
Further anomalies will trigger procedural hold.
```

"Procedural hold?" Max said.

Munch's shoulders sank.

"They can freeze your run and force a dock review."

"Mid-dive?"

"Mid-breath, if they feel theatrical."

Maxine tapped the draw panel.

"Then stop feeding them anomalies."

Max almost laughed.

"I'm not feeding them. I'm leaking."

Maxine met his gaze through the helmet reflection.

"Same result."

---

Round six started with less energy and more warnings.

```
[ROUND 6/10 READY]
Energy: 63/85
Procedural hold risk: ELEVATED
Choose action.
```

Card draw:

```
1) REPAIR
2) SHIELD (REINFORCE)
3) EXTRACT
```

No SCAN.
No SCAVENGE.

The system had done the math and decided Max didn't deserve curiosity.

Munch was already doing her own math.

"Shield. Then repair. Then maybe extract if we can pull enough."

Maxine shook his head.

"Extract early. Low energy means bad roll control."

Max looked between them.

He could feel the loop now.
Choice.
Consequences.
Bill.
Choice.

Groundhog day with better UI and worse weather.

He selected SHIELD.

The field came up thin, weaker than before, flickering at the edge of readiness.

```
[SHIELDS]
1 (unstable)
```

The derelict rolled and exposed a section of plating stamped with old serial characters.

Half of it was gone.

The part that remained read:

...RAINF...

Max's breath caught.

Rainf…

The rest of the plate sheared away and spun out of view before he could finish the word.

He didn't know why the letters mattered.

They mattered anyway.

A procedural hold warning slammed into view.

```
[PROCEDURAL HOLD WARNING]
Unscheduled attention event incoming.
Stand by for compliance review.
```

"No, no, no," Munch said, fingers flying over her wrist display.

"Can you cancel it?" Max asked.

"Can I cancel gravity?"

Maxine reached behind the seat and pulled a maintenance panel half-open, exposing a knot of cables and relay blocks.

"I can buy you seconds."

"By doing what?"

"By making the scanner dumb."

Maxine jammed a flat tool into the relay cluster and twisted.

The cockpit lights flickered.

V.A.L.U. crackled in and out.

"...pliance... review... remain calm... remain..."

The hold pane stalled at ninety percent.

Then ninety-one.

Then ninety-two.

"Three seconds," Maxine said.

"For what?"

"For a legal action."

Max looked at the cards.

EXTRACT was legal.
REPAIR was legal.

He hit EXTRACT.

The rig engaged.

The hold bar jumped to ninety-eight.

```
[BREACH CHECK]
Rolling...
```

The die appeared as the hold bar hit ninety-nine.

Die.
Bar.
Die.
Bar.

The die landed first.

```
[RESULT]
39/100
Threshold: 35
BREACH AVOIDED
```

Hold hit one hundred.

And froze there.

Nothing happened for a beat.

Then everything happened at once.

The cockpit flooded with overlapping panes.

```
[SALVAGE SECURED]
Scrap +87
Metal +1
XP +13
```

```
[PROCEDURAL HOLD]
Dock return required.
Control authority partially transferred.
```

```
[AUDIT FLAG]
Relay tampering probable.
```

Munch closed her eyes for one second like she was storing rage for later.

Maxine yanked the tool from the relay block and slapped the panel shut.

"We move," he said.

"Where?"

"Back to the mouth."

The skiff turned on autopriority routing.

Not auto-pilot.
Auto-priority.

The difference was legal.

The difference was that you still touched the controls while the ship ignored you.

---

Dock Ring C had changed by the time they got back.

Security lights were on.

Not red.
Blue-white.

Corporate emergency color: calm panic.

A lane had opened through the center of the ring, marked by portable barrier drones and stenciled arrows:

COMPLIANCE REVIEW FAST TRACK

Max laughed, once.

"Fast track."

Munch didn't.

"Get your story straight."

"What story?"

"That you followed prompts, used approved tactics, and definitely did not touch restricted interfaces."

Maxine added, "And if they ask about relay noise, blame old wiring."

"There is old wiring?"

"There is always old wiring."

The skiff docked hard enough to rattle the HUD.

Canopy unsealed.

Cold air.
Hot lights.
No time.

Two compliance officers were waiting at the hatch.

Not drones.
People.

White-black suits with Company insignia at the collar and mirrored visors that hid everything except how little they cared.

Officer One held a tablet slab.

Officer Two held nothing.

Officer Two was worse.

"Asset #864," Officer One said.

"Max," Max said before he could stop himself.

"Asset #864," Officer One repeated, tapping the slab once. "You are flagged for procedural hold review and anomaly-linked audit escalation. Step forward."

Max stepped down from the hatch.

Munch moved to flank him.

Officer Two raised a hand.

"Only the flagged asset."

Maxine stepped in anyway.

"He doesn't walk with you alone."

Officer Two turned his visor toward Maxine.

"You will remain behind the barrier."

Maxine smiled with no warmth.

"Then lower the barrier."

Officer One sighed like paperwork had learned to breathe.

"Do not make this a conduct issue."

Munch touched Maxine's arm.

Just once.

Maxine stopped moving.

Not obedient.
Strategic.

Max stepped into the marked lane.

The barriers hummed to life on either side.

The sound reminded him of electric toothbrushes and prison doors.

They walked him twenty meters to a glass review bay set into the ring wall.

Inside: one table, three chairs, one camera dome, one wall panel pulsing with the Company logo.

Officer Two stayed by the door.

Officer One sat, placed the slab on the table, and spoke in neutral cadence.

"State your designation."

Max stared.

"Asset #864."

"State your understanding of today's hold event."

Max looked at the slab.

It displayed his round timeline in ruthless detail:

Round 4: liquidation side effect active.
Round 5: anomaly stream block selected.
Round 6: procedural hold warning, relay disturbance, extract during hold transition.

No feelings.
No context.
No fear.

Just actions.

"I followed prompts," Max said.

Officer One nodded once.

"And did you access any restricted interface layers during rounds four through six?"

Max's pulse thudded in his ears.

He thought of the menu.
The options.
Return.

"No."

Officer One's tablet flickered.

```
[DISCREPANCY]
Residual traces present.
```

Officer One didn't react.

"Did you hear anomalous comms during active operation?"

Max hesitated.

Officer Two shifted at the door.

Max said, "Static."

Officer One tapped again.

"Static is expected. Clarify content."

Max's jaw tightened.

"Incomprehensible."

Another flicker.

```
[DISCREPANCY]
Audio event 'debug' registered.
```

Officer One folded his hands.

"Lying is billable."

Max looked up.

"Then maybe your system should stop hearing things through my skull."

Officer Two stepped forward.

Officer One held up a hand without looking.

"Do not escalate."

To Max:

"You are not being accused. You are being calibrated."

"Feels accusatory."

"Feelings are not in scope."

Max laughed, short and ugly.

"Of course not."

Officer One turned the slab so Max could see the next pane.

```
[CALIBRATION OPTIONS]
1) ACCEPT CONDUCT PENALTY
2) ACCEPT COGNITIVE STABILIZATION PACK
3) CONSENT TO DEEP TRACE REVIEW
```

Max read option three and felt his throat close.

Deep trace meant menus.
Voices.
Whatever lived in the leak.

If he consented, they'd know.

If he refused, they'd punish.

Option two looked harmless until the tooltip opened:

```
[COGNITIVE STABILIZATION PACK]
Effect: Suppresses anomalous interface bleed for 3 cycles.
Side effects: memory artifact loss probable.
```

Memory artifact loss.

Max thought of letters on a broken hull plate.

...RAINF...

Thought of rooftop rain.
Thought of debugging reality.

Hard-won pieces.

He wasn't giving them away.

"Option one," Max said.

Officer One nodded and tapped.

```
[CONDUCT PENALTY APPLIED]
- XP gain reduced 20% for next 2 rounds
- Card rarity ceiling reduced for next draw
- Audit sensitivity remains HIGH
```

Max didn't breathe for a second.

That was the cheapest option.

That was the expensive one.

Officer One stood.

"Review complete."

Officer Two opened the bay door.

"Return to assigned operations."

Max stepped out into Dock Ring C and almost laughed at how quickly the world resumed not caring.

Forklifts moved.
Alarms looped.
People hurried.

Munch and Maxine were waiting by the barrier.

Maxine spoke first.

"How bad?"

"XP tax. Rarity cap. High audit stays."

Munch flinched like he'd listed injuries.

"Did they offer stabilization?"

Max looked at her.

"How did you know that?"

Munch glanced away.

"Because they offered it to me once."

"You take it?"

She took a long breath through her nose.

"No."

"Why?"

"Because I was afraid they were selling peace by the kilogram."

Maxine gestured at the launch lanes.

"Debrief later. Round seven won't wait."

Max stared at him.

"You just watched me get audited."

"And now you get card draw."

"You're impossible."

"I'm employed."
