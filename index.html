FILE SS-Ω RULE

Severance bought you sleep. Sleep sold you forward.

Sector Scavengers

You wore the lanyard until AI ate the roadmap and your badge stopped scanning. The exit packet was almost kind: cryo until abundance, skip the ugly middle, wake into a gentle future. The future never arrived. Your contract did, touring balance sheets while you stayed on ice. Centuries later you are not an alum. You are debt, markdown, unhealthy inventory shuffled dock to dock until someone needed sinew in vacuum and your tab already had interest.

They wake you to a screen that already hates you: a number with too many zeroes before your knees unlock. Your job is derelicts in the black while company droids hang in the feed, snarky as a helpdesk and precise as tax law. They only love what you declare. Everything else is yours to smuggle, spend, or explain later. None of their paperwork keeps your hull closed when a card decides today is the day the metal screams. The next panel states the rules in plain language. Same truth, just louder.

How it plays

Laws of the loop

You stage every salvage run from the hub. Inside a derelict the run is a card game with teeth: a fixed hand size, a fuel gauge, and a hull that does not care about your intent. The bullets below are the non-negotiables. Card text, events, and toys color inside those lines.

  • You always hold three cards. Not two, not four. Three.
  • Energy is what you spend to play a card out of that hand. When the tank is empty, so are your options. And you must end your turn.
  • Each wreck rolls its own room count in secret. You learn how deep it goes by spending real actions: cards, skills, and tools can reveal rooms until the map stops lying.
    • If you walk into the end of the last room and still have not extracted, the game counts that as death. No overtime.
  • Every card carries a chance to damage the ship you are standing in. Stack enough hits on the hull and you die; the run ends there.
  • The campaign win is blunt: pay off the debt.
  • Declared credits go to the boss to pay off your debt.
  • Smuggled credits never touch the boss ledger. Off-book money builds the hub itself: new construction, crew pulled from cryo, research into tools, and ships upgraded or patched so the next breach is someone else's problem.
  • Every 3 ships you raid triggers a debt payment equal to 20% of your total debt. Miss 3 payments, and the corporation recycles your salvage skiff with you in it.
  • Any death feeds the void: you earn void experience that unlocks new cards for the next attempt.
