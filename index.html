Prologue · The lanyard

You wore the lanyard until AI ate the roadmap and your badge stopped scanning.

Caption The exit packet was almost kind: cryo until abundance, skip the ugly middle, wake into a gentle future. The future never arrived. Your contract did, touring balance sheets while you stayed on ice.

Prologue · Inventory

Centuries later you are not an alum. You are debt, markdown, unhealthy inventory.

Caption Shuffled dock to dock until someone needed sinew in vacuum and your tab already had interest.

Prologue · The feed

They wake you to a screen that already hates you: a number with too many zeroes before your knees unlock.

Caption Your job is derelicts in the black while company droids hang in the feed, snarky as a helpdesk and precise as tax law. They only love what you declare. Everything else is yours to smuggle, spend, or explain later. None of their paperwork keeps your hull closed when a card decides today is the day the metal screams.

How it plays

Laws of the loop

You stage every salvage run from the hub. Inside a derelict the run is a card game with teeth: a fixed hand size, a fuel gauge, and a hull that does not care about your intent. The panels below restate the non-negotiables in briefing form, same mechanics, louder line.

SS-Ω Field strip · Laws of the loop

Beat 01 · Hand

Three in the hand. That is the whole deal. Count them again if you need drama.

Mechanic You always hold three cards. Not two, not four. Three.

Beat 02 · Energy

Tank reads empty, you are done spending for the turn. No stalling, no miracles off-meter.

Mechanic Energy is what you spend to play a card out of that hand. When the tank is empty, so are your options. And you must end your turn.

Beat 03 · Map depth

The wreck does not show you its depth for free. You spend real actions until the map stops lying.

Mechanic Each wreck rolls its own room count in secret. You learn how deep it goes by spending real actions: cards, skills, and tools can reveal rooms until the map stops lying.

  • If you walk into the end of the last room and still have not extracted, the game counts that as death. No overtime.
Beat 04 · Hull

Cards bite the hull you are standing in. Stack enough hits and the run ends in the black.

Mechanic Every card carries a chance to damage the ship you are standing in. Stack enough hits on the hull and you die; the run ends there.

Beat 05 · Debt

Win condition is ugly and honest: zero the debt or stay a line item.

Mechanic The campaign win is blunt: pay off the debt.

Declared credits Declared credits go to the boss to pay off your debt.

Beat 06 · Smuggled ledger

What you do not declare stays yours, hub concrete, cryo keys, tools, hull patches. The company never sees that stack.

Mechanic Smuggled credits never touch the boss ledger. Off-book money builds the hub itself: new construction, crew pulled from cryo, research into tools, and ships upgraded or patched so the next breach is someone else's problem.

Beat 07 · Three-raid clock

Every third raid the ledger screams for twenty percent. Miss three of those payments and they scrap the skiff with you still in the harness.

Mechanic Every 3 ships you raid triggers a debt payment equal to 20% of your total debt. Miss 3 payments, and the corporation recycles your salvage skiff with you in it.

Beat 08 · Void XP

Die once, feed the void once. It remembers. Next thaw you get new angles.

Mechanic Any death feeds the void: you earn void experience that unlocks new cards for the next attempt.
