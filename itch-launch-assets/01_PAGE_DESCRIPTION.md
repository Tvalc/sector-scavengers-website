START

**You signed away the ugly centuries with a cryo severance package.**
**You woke up owing a corporation that barely remembers your name.**

Sector Scavengers: Signal & Salvage is a darkly funny salvage roguelite where every run is a pressure cooker. You enter derelict ships, make tactical card choices under rising risk, and decide exactly how far to push before the hull gives out.

This demo is a **free, browser-playable open run**, soft-capped at three hours of total session time. Long enough to feel the full salvage loop, declared vs. smuggled economy, and the moment a single greedy card decides today is the day the metal screams.

## What you can do in the demo

- **Run real expeditions.** Three cards in the hand, an energy meter that ends your turn for you, and a hull that does not care about your intent.
- **Push or leave.** Each wreck rolls its room count in secret. Spend real actions to reveal rooms, then extract before the run ends in the black.
- **Declared vs. smuggled.** What you bring back matters twice: declared credits pay the boss, smuggled value stays yours.
- **Three-raid clock.** Every three ships triggers a debt payment of twenty percent. Miss three payments, they scrap the skiff with you in it.
- **Void XP.** Die once, feed the void once. It remembers. Next thaw, you get new angles and new cards.

## What is NOT in this demo

This is a slice, not the campaign. The full hub progression, every crew lead, and the late-game corporate mandates land in the Steam release.

## Controls

- **Mouse:** click cards, click rooms, click extract.
- **Keyboard:** `1` `2` `3` to play hand slots, `Space` to end turn, `Esc` to pause.

## Known demo limits

- Soft session cap at three hours per browser session.
- Save state lives in browser storage. Clearing cookies wipes the run.
- Reported issues go to the devlog comments below. Please include your browser and OS.
