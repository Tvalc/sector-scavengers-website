Debt Protocol · Book 1 · Chapter 5

Trace Debt

The first sign the day was wrong came as a polite notification.

[LOCKER ACCESS ALERT]
Med Corridor B / Locker cluster
Routine inventory pass scheduled: 00:42:11

Max stared at the pane while standing in line for synthetic coffee that tasted like boiled wires.

Munch read it over his shoulder and said exactly one word.

"No."

Maxine looked up from his wrist display.

"How routine is routine?"

Munch's jaw tightened.

"Routine enough to include random confiscation."

Max dismissed the pane.

It returned immediately with the same timer.

00:42:03.

He dismissed it again.

00:41:58.

"It's pinning," he said.

Maxine took a slow breath.

"Then we move the cache."

Munch shook her head.

"Move now and we walk straight into pre-inventory patrol pattern."

"Leave it and we trust routine."

"We don't trust routine."

Max looked from one to the other, coffee packet going warm in his hand.

The station around them did what stations do:

Workers pushed carts.
Drones swept lanes.
People pretended not to overhear fear.

The overhead cycle board shifted lines in flat white text:

MID-CYCLE DEBT RECONCILIATION
QUALITY ASSURANCE SWEEPS

"That's inventory language," Munch said.

Maxine answered.

"It's seizure language."

Max didn't drink the coffee.

He poured it into a drain and watched brown foam spin away.

"How long to route to B corridor without touching monitored lanes?"

Munch pulled up map layers.

"Fastest clean path is twelve minutes if we split."

Maxine looked at Max.

"You're still tracked active. Your path pings no matter what."

"Then we use that."

Munch turned to him.

"Use how?"

"Maxine baits the tracker down main with me visible. You slip service and pull cache."

Maxine frowned.

"Reverse that. I'm tracked heavier."

"Exactly. You draw more eyes."

He was right and everyone hated it.

Munch slid her gaze between them, calculating risk like a person balancing glass on moving steel.

"Timer's running."

00:40:29.

"Fine," she said. "Maxine and Max go loud lane. I go spine C and hit B-17 from the back."

Max nodded.

The plan sat wrong in his chest, not because it was stupid, but because it repeated an old pattern: someone he cared about walking into danger while he stayed where the cameras could see him behave. Visibility was its own kind of cowardice here. He swallowed the objection. Objections without better tactics were just noise.

"What if locker is already tagged?"

Munch's expression didn't move.

"Then I leave with nothing and we pretend this was a walk."

Maxine clipped his gauntlets in place.

"And if you don't check in?"

Munch holstered her torch.

"You burn the route map and start a riot."

Maxine grunted.

"Reasonable."

Main lane was theater.

Max and Maxine walked shoulder to shoulder through the brightest part of Dock Ring C while Max's tracked token icon blinked on a three-second rhythm.

Blink.
Pause.
Blink.

Every blink felt like a finger in his back.

V.A.L.U. chimed at cheerful volume.

"Asset #864, thank you for maintaining visible route compliance!"

Max spoke through his teeth.

"You're welcome."

"Sarcasm detected. Logging engagement."

Maxine leaned close enough for private comms.

"Keep talking to it."

"Why?"

"Makes you look obedient."

"I'm not obedient."

"I know. We just need them to think you are for ten minutes."

They passed disbursement lane A.

A woman in a medic stripe suit was arguing with a kiosk while two drones recorded from opposite angles.

"I paid! I paid the minimum!"

The kiosk responded:

[PAYMENT ACKNOWLEDGED]
Additional stabilization surcharge due.

The woman slammed her fist into the glass.

The drones moved in.

Max kept his eyes forward for three strides. On the fourth he looked back, just long enough to fix her face in memory, the way you'd memorize a witness you might need later. Maxine didn't scold him for it. That was almost worse. It meant even small mercies had been priced into the plan.

He kept walking because stopping would be a statement.

He hated that he knew that now.

Maxine steered him around a cargo tug and into the open plaza beneath the claim board.

Sixteen slots floated.
Ten occupied.
Two flashing red.

An overhead announcement cut in:

"Attention assets: quality assurance sweep in Med Corridor B in thirty minutes. Please report personal storage claims proactively to reduce friction."

Max did not look at Maxine.

Maxine did not look at Max.

They both heard it.

"Can QA announce exact corridor like that?" Max asked.

"Only when they want panic traffic to reveal outliers."

"Great."

"Always."

They looped once around the claim board, then doubled back past Salvage Yard to burn clock and produce clean telemetry.

Max's tracked token changed color from blue to pale green.

[TRACKED ACTIVE]
Route regularity: improving

"I am going to punch the person who wrote this UI," Max said.

Maxine kept his voice flat.

"Take a number."

Munch moved through service spine C like she had built it herself.

He only heard her in short private bursts in his left audio channel.

"Crossed junction."
"One drone overhead, no pause."
"At ladder drop."

Then silence for thirty seconds.

Max's pulse climbed.

"Munch?"

Static.

Maxine didn't break stride.

"She mutes during fine work."

Twenty seconds later:

"At locker row. Two workers nearby. Waiting cycle."

Max exhaled.

Maxine said, "Good."

Munch:

"Locker cluster scanner is running full pass already. Early start."

Maxine stopped dead.

"How early."

"Now."

Max looked at the timer pane still pinned in his corner.

00:31:02.

"They moved it up."

Munch's voice dropped.

"Locker B-17 is flagged yellow."

Max's mouth went dry.

"Can you pull it?"

"Trying."

He could hear faint tool sounds through the channel.

Click.
Metal tap.
Breath.

Then:

"No."

Maxine's shoulders tightened.

"No what."

"Panel's been replaced. New anti-tamper mesh inside. If I force it, local alarm."

Max's hands curled into fists.

"Then we lost it."

"Not yet."

Munch sounded like she was moving.

"There's a maintenance vent behind the locker wall. If I can reach from rear conduit, I might pull the shim blind."

Maxine nodded once, even though she couldn't see.

"Do it."

"Thirty seconds."

Max counted heartbeats.

At twelve, V.A.L.U. interrupted.

"Asset #864, your route regularity has improved by 14%! Keep up the responsible behavior!"

Max wanted to bite through his own tongue.

Maxine hissed, "Play nice."

"Thanks, V.A.L.U.," Max said.

"You're welcome!"

At twenty-seven heartbeats, Munch came back on channel, breathing hard.

"Got it."

Max stopped walking.

"Say again."

"Got the shim. Almost dropped it into coolant return. It smells like dead batteries and shame, but it's in my glove."

Maxine actually smiled for half a second.

"Route out?"

"Complicated."

"How complicated."

"Two QA officers entered corridor. They're scanning random lockers manually."

Max's HUD flashed.

[PROXIMITY ALERT]
Linked teammate anomaly risk: elevated

"It can see your risk now," Max said.

"It can always see risk," Maxine said. "Now it monetizes it in real time."

Munch cut in.

"Need a distraction in three minutes or I get trapped between scanner lanes."

Maxine looked toward the claim board and then up at a hanging cable crane with overloaded cargo.

"I can give you noise."

"Not fire," Munch said immediately.

"No fire."

"No injuries."

"I said no fire."

Maxine cracked his neck.

"Max, with me."

Maxine's plan was simple and therefore terrible.

At the edge of claim board lane, he pointed at a red-flashing derelict slot and said, loudly:

"That fighter is mis-tagged. It's stealing my claim line."

A nearby asset turned.

"No it's not."

Maxine stepped forward.

"Read the registry hash."

"I can't from here."

"Then stop talking."

Within fifteen seconds three more people had stopped to watch.

Within twenty-five seconds two drones had rolled in for dispute recording.

Within forty seconds V.A.L.U. appeared on public speakers:

"Attention assets! Please resolve claim disagreements using approved civility pathways."

Maxine leaned in harder, voice rising exactly to the level that looked authentic and drew supervisors.

"Approved civility can kiss vacuum. That tag is wrong."

The crowd swelled.

Drones multiplied.

Max stood one step behind Maxine and played confused muscle.

His tracked token stayed green.

[TRACKED ACTIVE]
Conflict proximity detected.
No direct aggression logged.

Good.

"Passing scanner lane two."

Five seconds later:

"Past QA officers."

Ten seconds:

"Out of B corridor. Moving to yard route."

Maxine kept yelling for another full minute, then let a supervisor drone "win" by offering to file a formal complaint.

He accepted.

"Fine. File it."

The crowd dispersed instantly, disappointed and relieved.

Maxine stepped back beside Max and lowered his voice.

"Where is she."

"Moving yard route."

"Good."

A new pane appeared in Max's corner.

[CONDUCT NOTICE]
You remained non-violent during a conflict event.
Behavior credit: +1

Max laughed, genuinely this time.

"Behavior credit. Amazing."

Maxine rolled his eyes.

"Don't spend it all in one place."

They regrouped behind recycler stack two, out of primary sightlines but still in noise.

Munch arrived two minutes late, breathing hard, visor streaked with coolant mist.

She held up her glove.

Inside, nested in folded mesh cloth, was the storage shim.

Max almost sagged.

"You did it."

"Don't praise me until we verify files."

Maxine looked at the corridor.

"Not here."

"Safe room?"

"Too obvious."

Munch shook her head.

"We use Marta."

Max blinked.

"Marta? Yard ops Marta?"

"Marta has a dead printer terminal with no net uplink and old physical ports."

"Why would she help."

Munch gave him a look.

"Because she hates mixed payout math almost as much as she hates management."

"That's not a reason."

"In this sector it is."

Marta did not greet them.

She was elbow-deep in a sorter tray full of bent tech components and didn't look up when they approached.

"Not buying. Not selling. Come back on cycle quarter."

Munch put both palms on the counter.

"Need your printer terminal."

Marta finally glanced up.

"No."

Maxine leaned one arm on the side rail.

"We'll pay."

"No."

Max slid his tracked token icon into low visibility mode with a tap and tried his luck.

"Please."

Marta stared at him for a long second, then snorted.

"Please costs extra."

Munch pulled a sealed tube from her belt pouch and set it on the counter.

Marta's eyes flicked to it.

"What is that."

"Pre-collapse thermal paste. Real silver suspension."

Marta went still.

"You're lying."

Munch unscrewed the cap enough for the smell to hit.

Marta's expression changed from skepticism to hunger.

"...Fine," she said. "Three minutes. If drones ask, you're here for valuation dispute."

She jerked her chin toward a side bay and keyed a hidden panel.

A narrow room opened behind stacked crates.

Inside sat an old print-and-log station with a cracked keyboard, dead fan, and a side slot that looked exactly like the shim's form factor.

"Three minutes," Marta repeated. "Then I never saw you."

Munch inserted the shim.

Maxine swore.

"Out. Now."

Lens swept.

"I told you valuation mismatch isn't my problem! Take your complaint to lane F!"

"Then lane C can kiss my actuator!"

"Never again."

"Until next time."

Good.
