Morning in Sector 7 didn’t arrive with sun.

It arrived with receipts.

Some receipts were paper.

Some were hashes.

Some were blood pressure graphs pretending to be morale.

Max preferred the hashes.

Not yet.

The service spine didn’t brighten.

It relented.

Max’s boots found rhythm on grated decking: not fast, not slow.

The rhythm of a man who knew cameras loved panic and hated patience.

Maxine walked like a door.

DEFERRED, the state had said.

Deferred was a word that pretended to be mercy.

Max knew better.

Deferred was a loan with teeth.

Max had learned the taste of that word in smaller bites: the cryo glass he couldn't open, the strut he didn't report, the tether he didn't cut. Deferred always meant someone else paid interest first, until the account rolled over and your name was next.
