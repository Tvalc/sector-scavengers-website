Preliminary

They moved before fear could become a meeting.

Maxine took the lead through bunk corridor K with his shoulders slightly turned, making himself wider than the lane and harder to separate from the two people behind him.

Munch walked half a step behind Max with the portable pad under her arm and her face set in the blank expression workers wore when they had accepted a thing they had not accepted.

Max walked center.
Summons pane burning at HUD edge.

[CYCLE OPEN]
Asset #864 report immediately.
Special Review Intake: PATH C PRELIMINARY
Failure to report escalates enforcement.

The text stayed even when he blinked.

They reached the first choke point where corridor K fed into main ring transit.

Two patrol drones stood on opposite walls scanning badges.

Maxine slowed without stopping.

"No running. No whispering. No sudden turns."

Max nodded once.

Munch said quietly, "If they pull him at the choke, we trigger release."

Maxine: "Only if he's physically separated."

Max: "If they ask me to step aside?"

Maxine: "You step. We step with."

Simple rules.
No comfort.

They passed scan.

One drone paused on Max, lens tightening for one extra second.

Then:

"Proceed."

They proceeded.

Special Review Intake did not exist yesterday.

Today it occupied a converted logistics office near Compliance Suite 3 with fresh signage and cheap privacy film on glass walls.

PATH C PRELIMINARY
CONDUCTED WITH CARE

The word care had no right to be there.

A receptionist in clean gray uniform looked up from a terminal and smiled with the exact amount of empathy approved by policy.

"Asset #864."

"Yes."

"Please proceed to room P-C1."

"Companions are not required."

"Companions are present."

"No."

"Then clause does not apply."

Maxine: "He contests variance. We remain in corridor."

"Corridor wait permitted. No interference."

Max looked at both of them.

not leaving.

He stepped into room P-C1.

Door sealed behind him with a soft magnetic click.

On the other side of that sound, Maxine and Munch still existed, he knew it the way you knew a hand stayed raised after a wave ended, but knowing didn't change the physics: for the next minutes, he was alone with whatever script the room had been built to read.

His HUD tried to offer a checklist. He minimized it. Some fears shouldn't be gamified.

"Feedback logged."

Halden had tidy hair, tidy collar, and eyes that had read too many summaries about people like Max without ever meeting one.

"You know why you're here."

Max answered honestly.

"Because you have a spreadsheet for dissent."

Halden's mouth twitched, not a smile.

"Because your profile indicates repeated conduct variance, telemetry anomalies, unauthorized zone adjacency, and elevated debt stress. Preliminary review determines whether enhanced retention pathways are appropriate."

Enhanced retention pathways.

Max leaned back.

"You turned kidnapping into corporate copy."

"Let's begin with straightforward questions."

Question 3: Do you report any unauthorized contact attempts from unknown entities?
YES / NO

His chest tightened.

Unknown messages.
Hatch helper.
Voice in node chamber.

He chose NO.

The lie sat in his mouth like metal. It wasn't courage, it was triage. If he named the hatch helper here, the station wouldn't thank him for honesty; it would turn honesty into a map and follow it to someone else's throat.

Halden watched the selections and made a note.

"Question four is open response. In your words, why has your telemetry been unstable."

Max laughed once.

"Because your station is built on dead metal, bad signal, and desperate people."

Halden typed.

"And yet most assets maintain stable telemetry."

"Most assets aren't on your mirror list."

Halden's typing stopped.

One beat of silence.

"What mirror list."

"The one with my name and other versions of it."

Halden expression did not change.

"You are making claims that suggest stress-related patterning."
