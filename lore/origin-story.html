Attached is the orientation strip we use when someone asks how we got here.

It skips the polite version. It still cannot fit every footnote. Treat it as a file you are not supposed to forward.

SS-Ω Lore strip · Wake into ledger

Panel 01 · Layoffs

In 2026 the layoffs stop pretending to be temporary.

Caption Internal tools already draft the decks, file the tickets, and answer the standups. The all hands is short. The slide deck is long. By lunch the badge printers go quiet.

Panel 02 · Exit path

HR calls the packet an exit path.

Caption Cryo sits in the same row as COBRA on a benefits table nobody reads until they are already tired. Sleep through the scarcity years, the brochure says. Wake when the world is gentle again. Initial here. Initial here. Initial here.

Panel 03 · Vaults as spectacle

The vaults turn into something you photograph.

Caption Glass towers full of frost and money. Living heirs stand beside sleeping ancestors and post both like proof of lineage. Tours run on a schedule. The cold smells expensive on purpose.

Panel 04 · New paperwork

Scarcity does not vanish. It only learns new paperwork.

Caption Brochure ink fades. The utopia date keeps moving. What used to be sold as a suite upgrade becomes a rated instrument, bundled, tranched, handed down to children who never asked to inherit a bill for someone else’s nap.

Panel 05 · Warehouse

Someone notices a shelf can hold a person the same way it holds a crate.

Caption Pods stack where overflow inventory used to sit. Labels list names the way they list SKUs. The first shift supervisor treats the cold like logistics, because that is what the spreadsheet calls it.

Panel 06 · Bad inventory

The count goes wrong slowly, then all at once.

Caption Mold in a gasket line. A coolant loop that never gets funded. Fees for power, for air, for staying asleep. The asset stays breathing. The balance climbs anyway.

Panel 07 · Sold to salvage

A broker prints a lot that includes warm bodies still under contract.

Caption The winning bid carries a logo you do not recognize and a jurisdiction you cannot find on a map. Signatures happen in orbit where local law is mostly custom and handshake. The transfer stamp lands. The debt has a new owner. The people attached to it get a manifest number and a berth.

Panel 08 · Thaw

Thaw alarms go off in batches. Millions sit up into the same bright UI, same total, same due date.

Caption Orientation is a locker combination. Training is a shrug. The suit seals. The airlock clock starts. Someone already logged you onto a salvage roster while you were still vomiting thaw gel.

Panel 09 · Debris field

The bus run drops them at the junk tide line. The first wreck is a wall of bent metal and old paint.

Caption They burn fuel before they earn scrap. Every choice costs something you can measure. A few figure out the math early. Most learn it when the tank needle twitches and the hull starts talking back.

Panel 10 · Boarding

The derelict’s mouth is a dark rectangle cut into a hull that used to belong to someone proud.

Caption They go through because the company channel keeps counting down and because standing still also costs money. The first room looks empty the way a stage looks empty before the lights come up. Then the suit readouts twitch and you hear metal settle somewhere you did not touch.

Panel 11 · Explore

The map lies until you pay it in actions.

Caption Some doors pay out. Some doors bill you in blood pressure and hull dents. The loot sits where something else already learned the layout.

End attachment.

This strip is backstory rail for Sector Scavengers: Signal & Salvage. The game starts where the debt gets personal.
